Clue 5: The Stories They Dream About

"Look closer at the stories themselves; what do different lands dream about?"

Research Question: What storytelling themes dominate in different regions, and how do these vary by genre?

Deciphering the Clue

This clue invites us to explore the stories told across different parts of the world. What do these stories reveal about each region? What kinds of narratives are shared, and how do they differ? To answer these questions, we need a clear and systematic way to identify and compare the main ideas within these stories.

Method

To uncover the topics in a collection of texts, we use Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) . LDA is a method designed specifically for topic modeling . It looks at the patterns of words in documents and groups words that frequently occur together into topics . The idea is simple: each document is made up of a mix of topics, and each topic is described by a specific set of words.

By applying LDA to movie plots, we can figure out which topics are most common in films from different countries. This helps us see what kinds of stories people focus on and how they vary from one place to another.

We‚Äôll start by applying LDA to look at movies in a broad, general way , analyzing themes across all genres. This gives us a big-picture view of storytelling worldwide. But is that enough? Can broad themes really capture the cultural differences we‚Äôre looking for, or do we need to go further?

This first step will help us decide. Once we‚Äôve identified the general themes, we can narrow our focus to specific genres to dig deeper and see what makes each region unique.

General LDA Analysis

Like many machine learning models, LDA needs tuning to get the best results. In our case, the key parameter to tune is the number of topics . A good way to find the right number is to try models with different topic counts‚Äîsay, from 2 to 5‚Äîand choose the one that gives us the most coherent topics . Coherence measures how interpretable and meaningful the topics are.

But there‚Äôs a catch, isn‚Äôt there? Think about it: in cinema, there are countless topics‚Äîthankfully! So as we increase the number of topics, we might find that the coherence score keeps improving. Why? Because with more topics, the model gets more specific, breaking down themes into smaller, clearer pieces.

However, a more coherent model doesn‚Äôt always mean a better one. If we choose too many topics, the results become so specific that they‚Äôre hard to interpret. How are we supposed to understand 20 topics a make an interesting comparison?

To strike a balance, let‚Äôs focus on finding the most coherent model within the range of 2 to 5 topics. This will give us themes that are specific enough to be meaningful but broad enough to interpret easily.

No doubt, 4 topics is the perfect compromise between interpretability and coherence of the LDA model.
